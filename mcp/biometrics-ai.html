Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about biometric governance

What is biometric AI governance?
Biometric AI governance is the systematic oversight and compliance management of artificial intelligence systems that process biometric data (facial recognition, fingerprints, voice, iris scans). It ensures these systems comply with regulations like BIPA, GDPR, and the EU AI Act while preventing bias, protecting privacy, and maintaining ethical standards.
How does the facial recognition auditor detect bias?
The facial recognition auditor analyzes system performance across demographic groups to identify accuracy disparities. It measures false positive/negative rates by age, gender, and ethnicity, then compares results to industry standards and NIST FRVT benchmarks to detect discrimination patterns.
What regulations does the compliance checker cover?
The biometric compliance checker validates against BIPA (Biometric Information Privacy Act), GDPR Article 9 (biometric data processing), EU AI Act high-risk classifications, CCPA/CPRA biometric provisions, LGPD Article 5 (sensitive personal data), and emerging state-level biometric laws.
Can I track biometric data retention and deletion?
Yes. The biometric data classifier tracks the complete lifecycle of biometric information, including collection, storage, processing, and deletion. It monitors retention policies, alerts when retention periods expire, and verifies destruction compliance with regulatory requirements.
Does this cover voice and fingerprint biometrics?
Yes. The biometrics-ai MCP covers all biometric modalities including facial recognition, voice biometrics, fingerprints, iris/retina scans, and behavioral biometrics. Each has specific compliance and bias detection requirements that are addressed comprehensively.
How often should I run compliance audits?
We recommend running compliance audits at least quarterly, or whenever you update your biometric systems, change data retention policies, or expand to new demographics. High-risk applications (law enforcement, border control) should audit monthly or continuously.
