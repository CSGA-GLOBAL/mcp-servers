Frequently Asked Questions

How does ProofOf AI create tamper-proof audit trails?

ProofOf AI uses SHA-256 cryptographic hashing combined with blockchain anchoring. Each AI decision is hashed with its full context and timestamp, then cryptographically linked to previous decisions. Any modification to historical records would change the hash, breaking the chain and flagging tampering. Blockchain anchoring provides external verification of the proof chain.

Which regulatory frameworks does ProofOf AI support?

ProofOf AI generates compliance proofs for 40+ frameworks including ISO 42001, SOX (financial), GDPR (EU), CCPA (California), HIPAA (healthcare), FDA 21 CFR Part 11 (medical devices), FINRA (securities), PCI DSS (payments), and industry-specific standards. Each proof includes framework-specific evidence requirements and attestation formats.

Can regulators verify proofs without accessing our AI systems?

Yes. ProofOf AI generates self-verifying cryptographic proofs that auditors can validate independently. Regulators receive a proof bundle containing decision hashes, timestamps, evidence chains, and blockchain anchors. They can verify the cryptographic signatures and audit trail integrity without accessing the original AI systems, training data, or infrastructure.

What's the performance impact on AI inference?

Minimal impact. Decision capture and hashing adds <1ms latency. Proof generation happens asynchronously and can be batched. ProofOf AI integrates as middleware in decision pipelines without blocking inference. Enterprise deployments report <0.5% performance overhead with cryptographic verification enabled.

How does ProofOf AI handle high-volume decision logging?

ProofOf AI uses batch verification and Merkle tree aggregation to handle millions of decisions daily. Decisions are grouped into blocks, cryptographically aggregated, then anchored to blockchain. Enterprise deployments support 100k+ decisions/second with streaming audit logs and real-time proof verification dashboards.
